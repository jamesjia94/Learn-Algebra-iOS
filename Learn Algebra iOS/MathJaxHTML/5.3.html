Adding and Subtracting Rational Expressions

Adding rational expressions is just like adding fractions; you need to have the same denominator. Look at this example:
\[\frac{5x}{(2x-1)}+\frac{x^2}{2}\]
In order to add these, we need to make the denominators the same, so we multiply the first term by 2 and the second one by \((2x-1)\). Then we just add. That leaves our answer as:
\[\frac{10x+x^2 (2x-1)}{2(2x-1)}\]
The same can be done with subtraction. Just remember to always equalize the denominators.
