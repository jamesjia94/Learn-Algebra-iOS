Terms

5.1

Rational Expression: an algebraic expression that can be expressed in the form \(\frac{a}{b}\); basically, anything that can be written as a fraction

Restriction: something that a variable cannot equal for the function to be defined. For example, in the fraction \(\frac{5}{x}\), the restriction is that \(x\) cannot equal 0, because you cannot divide by 0

5.4

Complex Fraction: a fraction where either the numerator or denominator (or both) are fractions

Sample Solutions

5.2

Multiplying Rational Expressions:

Multiply \(\frac{1}{2x}\) and \(\frac{x+5}{8}\)

Multiply the numerators: \(1*\left(x+5\right)=x+5\)

Multiply the denominators: \(2x*8=16x\)

Put the numerator expression above the denominator expression: \(\frac{x+5}{16x}\)

Answer: \(\frac{x+5}{16x}\)

Dividing Rational Expressions:

Divide \(\frac{x}{2}\) and \(\frac{5}{2+2x}\)

Reverse the second term: \(\frac{2+2x}{5}\)

Multiply this new term and the first term: \(\frac{x}{2}*\frac{2+2x}{5}\)

Multiply the numerators: \(x*(2+2x)=2x+2x^2\)

Multiply the denominators: \(2*5=10\)

Put the numerator expression above the denominator expression: \(\frac{2x^2+2x}{10}\)

Answer: \(\frac{2x^2+2x}{10}\)

5.3

Adding Rational Expressions:

Add \(\frac{5}{x+1}\) and \(\frac{x}{4}\)

First, determine the common denominator. Since neither denominator has any factors, multiply the two together to create: \(4*(x+1)=4(x+1)\)

Multiply both terms by one: \(\frac{5}{x+1}*1+\frac{x}{4}*1\)

Change the 1 multiplied to the first term to supply the missing components of the common denominator we created in step one: \(\frac{4(x+1)}{x+1}=4\rightarrow1=\frac{4}{4}\rightarrow\frac{5}{x+1}*\frac{4}{4}+\frac{x}{4}*1\)

Change the 1 multiplied to the second term to supply the missing components of the common denominator we created in step one: \(\frac{4(x+1)}{4}=(x+1)\rightarrow1=\frac{x+1}{x+1}\rightarrow\frac{5}{x+1}*\frac{4}{4}+\frac{x}{4}*\frac{x+1}{x+1}\)

Simplify: \(\frac{5*4}{4(x+1)} + \frac{x(x+1)}{4(x+1)} \rightarrow \frac{5*4+x(x+1)}{4(x+1)} \rightarrow \frac{(x^2+x+20)}{4(x+1)}\)

Answer: \(\frac{x^2+x+20}{4x+4}\)

Subtracting Rational Expressions:

Subtract \(\frac{x-2}{2} and \frac{3}{4x}\)

First, determine the common denominator. Since the first term’s denominator is a factor of the second, use the second as the common denominator.

Multiply the first term by 1: \(\frac{x-2}{2}*1-\frac{3}{4x}\)

Change the 1 multiplied to the first term to supply the missing components of the common denominator we created in step one: \(\frac{x-2}{2}*\frac{2x}{2x}-\frac{3}{4x}\)

Simplify: \(\frac{2x(x-2)}{4x}-\frac{3}{4x}\rightarrow\frac{4x^2-4x}{4x}-\frac{3}{4x}\rightarrow\frac{4x^2-4x-3}{4x}\)

Answer: \(\frac{4x^2-4x-3}{4x}\)

5.4

Complex Rational Expressions:

Simplify \(\frac{1}{1+\frac{1}{1+\frac{1}{x}}}\)

First, simplify the bottommost denominator: \(1+\frac{1}{x}\rightarrow\frac{x}{x}+\frac{1}{x}\rightarrow\frac{x+1}{x}\)

This gives us: \(\frac{1}{1+\frac{1}{\frac{x+1}{x}}}\)

Simplify the new fraction: \(\frac{1}{\frac{x+1}{x}}\rightarrow1÷\frac{x+1}{x}\rightarrow1*\frac{x}{x+1}\rightarrow\frac{x}{x+1}\)

This gives us: \(\frac{1}{1+\frac{x}{x+1}}\)

Simplify the denominator: \(1+\frac{x}{x+1}\rightarrow\frac{x+1}{x+1}+\frac{x}{x+1}\rightarrow\frac{2x+1}{x+1}\)

This gives us: \(\frac{1}{\frac{2x+1}{x+1}}\)

Simplify the new fraction: \(\frac{1}{\frac{2x+1}{x+1}}\rightarrow1÷\frac{2x+1}{x+1}\rightarrow1*\frac{x+1}{2x+1}\rightarrow\frac{x+1}{2x+1}\)

Answer: \(\frac{x+1}{2x+1}\)
