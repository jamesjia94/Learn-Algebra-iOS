Terms

6.1

Factors: factors of a number x are number that, when multiplied together, equal x. For example, 5 and 2 are factors of 10 (2*5=10)

Factorization: the process of breaking a number up into factors

Prime number: a number whose only factors are 1 and itself.

Prime factorization: the factorization of a number that only contains prime factors.

Greatest Common Factor: the largest factors that two numbers have in common.

Relatively Prime: two numbers are relatively prime if their greatest common factor is 1.

Factoring by Grouping: a method to factor long polynomials by breaking it into smaller groups, then factoring each group separately.

6.3

Factoring \(a^2 + 2ab + b^2\): \(a^2 + 2ab + b^2 = (a+b)^2\)

Factoring \(a^2 - 2ab + b^2\): \(a^2 - 2ab + b^2 = (a-b)^2\)

Factoring \(a^2 - b^2\): \(a^2-b^2 = (a+b)(a-b)\)

Factoring \(a^3 + b^3\):\(a^3+b^3= (a+b)(a^2-ab+b^2)\)

Factoring \(a^3 - b^3\):\(a^3-b^3= (a-b)(a^2+ab+b^2)\)

Sample Solutions

6.1

Factoring a Monomial:

Find the prime factorization of \(6x^5\)

Break \(6x^5\) into its parts: \(6x^5 = 6 * x^5\)

Find the prime factorization of \(6\): \(6 = 3*2*1\)

Find the factorization of \(x^5\): \(x^5 = x * x * x * x * x\)

Multiply the two together: \(3 * 2 * x * x * x * x * x\)

Answer: \(3 * 2 * x * x * x * x * x\)

Factoring a Polynomial:

Factor \(12x+36\)

The GCF of \(12x\) and \(36\) is \(12\)

Divide both terms by the GCF, 12: \( \frac{12x}{12} = x, \frac{36}{12} = 3 \)

Multiply the new binomial formed with the previous terms by 12: \( 12 * (x + 3)\)

Answer: \(12(x+3)\)

Factoring by Grouping:

Factor \(2x^3+4x^2+6x+12\)

Group the first two terms together, and the last two together: \( (2x^3+4x^2) + (6x+12)\)

Factor the two binomials: \(2x^2(x+2) + 6(x+2)\)

Factor out the binomial \((x+2)\): \((x+2)(2x^2 + 6)\)

Check the newly created binomial for factors: \((x+2)(2(x^2 + 3))\)

Answer: \(2(x+2)(x^2 +3)\)

6.2

Factoring trinomials of the form \(x^2+bx+c\):

Factor \(x^2 + 5x + 6\)

Find factors of 6 that add up to equal \(5\): Factors of \(6\) = \(1, 2, 3, 6; 2 + 3 = 5\)

Create two binomials with these two factors:\((x+2)\) and \((x+3)\)

Answer: \((x+2)(x+3)\)

Factoring trinomials of the form \(ax^2+bx+c\):

Factor \(4x^2 + 10x + 6\)

Find the numerical factors of the first term: \(4x^2\)'s coefficient = \(4\); Factors of \(4\) = \(1, 2, 4\)

Find the numerical factors of the last term: Factors of \(6\) = \(1, 2, 3, 6\)

Multiply the factors of the first term and the factors of the second term such that they add up to equal \(10\): \(2 * 3 = 6; 2 * 2 = 4; 6 + 4 = 10\)

Create two binomials with these factors:\((2x+2)(2x+3)\)

Answer: \((2x+2)(2x+3)\)

Factoring a Sum of Cubes:

Factor \(x^3 + 8\)

Formula for a sum of cubes: \(a^3+b^3 = (a+b)(a^2-ab+b^2)\)

Find the cube root of the first term: \(\sqrt[3]{x^3}=x\)

Find the cube root of the second term: \(\sqrt[3]{8}=2\)

Use the formula: \((x+2)(x^2-2x+4)\)

Answer: \((x+2)(x^2-2x+4)\)
