Terms

2.1

Rectangular Coordinate System: gives any point on a plane a unique combination of x and y coordinates that specifies its location on the plane

X-axis: the horizontal line where the y coordinate is 0 on a graph. Points to the right of the y-axis are have positive x values and points to the left have negative x values

Y-axis: the vertical line where the x coordinate is 0 on a graph. Points above the x-axis have positive y values and points below the x-axis have negative y values.

Plane: the 2-dimensional surface that the x-axis and y-axis lie on.

Ordered Pair: a combination of a x coordinate and y coordinate in this form: (x,y) that identifies the location of a point

Midpoint: the point in the exact middle of two other points.

2.2

Intercept Method: a method of graphing where you find the x and y intercepts of an equation and connect them to form a line

X-intercept: the point where the line meets the x-axis

Y-intercept: the point where the line meets the y-axis

2.3

Slope: a ratio relating how quickly a line moves up or down to how quickly it moves left or right. Horizontal lines have a slope of 0, and vertical lines have an undefined slope.

2.5

Parallel Lines: different lines that have the same slope

Perpendicular Lines: lines that have slopes that are negative reciprocals

Negative Reciprocal: numbers that have opposite signs and are reciprocals. When multiplied together, they equal -1. Eg: \( \frac{2}{5}\)and \(-\frac{5}{2}\)are negative reciprocals

2.6

Function: a relation between two different variables, often expressed as x and y.

Domain: group of all the possible values of x for a function.

Range: group of all the possible values of y for a function.

Vertical Line Test: a test that determines whether a graph is a function or not. To use this test, move a imaginary vertical lines across the graph. If the graph ever intersects the line a two points at the same time, then it is not a function.
