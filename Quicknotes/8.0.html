Terms

8.1

Extracting the Root: Solving quadratic equations by isolating the squared term then square rooting both side of the equation.

8.2

Completing the Square: Solving quadratic equations by converting them into perfect squares, then extracting the root.

Perfect Square: A quadratic equation in the form \(ax^2+bx+c=0\) that can be written as the square of another term, such as \((x+y)^2\).

8.3

Discriminant: The part of the quadratic formula that tells you how many real solutions there are for a quadratic equation.

8.4

Vertex Form: A quadratic equation in the form \(y=a(x-h)^2+h\)

y-intercept: The point where the parabola intersects the \(y\)-axis.

x-intercept: The point (or points) where the parabola intersects the \(x\)-axis.

Formulas

8.3

Quadratic Formula: \(x=\frac{-b\pm \sqrt{b^2-4ac}}{2a}\)

Sample Solutions

8.1

Solve: \((\frac{1}{2})x^2-4=6\)

Isolate the squared term: \(\frac{1}{2}x^2=6+4\)

\(x^2=(6+4)*2 \\x^2=20\)

Square root both sides of the equation:\(x=\pm\sqrt{20}\)

Simplify:\(x=\pm2\sqrt{5}\)

8.2

Solve \(x^2-6x=16\)

Turn the equation into the form \(x^2+bx+c=0\): \(x^2-6x-16=0\)

Complete the square. Add or subtract to both sides so that \(c=(\frac{b}{2})^2\): \(x^2-6x-16=0\)

\((\frac{b}{2})^2=9 \\c+25=\frac{b}{2})^2 \\x^2-6x-16+25=25 \\x^2-6x+9=25\)

Simplfy:\(x^2-6x+9=25\)

\((x-3)^2=25\)

Extract the root:\(x-3=\pm\sqrt{25}=\pm5\)

\(\\x-3=5, x-3=-5 \\x=8,-2\)

8.3

Solve: \(3x^2+7x-20=0\)

Plug the values into the formula:\(x=\frac{-7\pm\sqrt{7^2-4*3*-20}}{2*3}\)

Simplfiy:\(x=\frac{-7\pm\sqrt{49+240}}{6} \\x=\frac{-7\pm\sqrt{289}}{6} \\x=\frac{-7\pm17}{6} \\x=\frac{10}{6},\frac{-24}{6} \\x=\frac{5}{3}, -4\)
