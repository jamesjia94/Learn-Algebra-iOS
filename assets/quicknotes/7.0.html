Terms

7.1

Square Root: The square root of a number is a number that, when multiplied by itself, equals some original numbers Eg. The square root of \(16\) is \(4\) (\(4*4=16\))

Radicand: A number that is under the radical sign. In exponent form, it is called the base

Cube Root: A number, when multiplied by itself three times, equals some original number. E.g The cube root of \(27\) is \(3\) (\(3*3*3=27\))

7.3

Radical Expression: Any algebraic expression that uses radicals

7.5

Rationalizing the Denominator: Getting rid of radicals in the denominator of a fraction by multiplying the top and bottom by the radical in the denominator

7.6

Radical Equations: Any equation that involves radicals

Squaring Property of Equality: If \(a=b\), then \(a^2=b^2\)

Sample Solutions

7.4

Adding Radical Expressions:

Simplify \(2\sqrt{20}+5\sqrt{5}+3\sqrt{45}\)

Simplify each radical: \(2*2\sqrt{5}+5\sqrt{5}+3*3\sqrt{5}\)

Add the coefficients: \(4\sqrt{5}+5\sqrt{5}+9\sqrt{5}\)

Answer: \(18\sqrt{5}\)

Subtracting Radical Expressions:

Simplify \(4\sqrt{27}-5\sqrt{12}\)

Simplify each radical: \(4*3\sqrt{3}+5*2\sqrt{3}\)

Subtract the coefficients: \(12\sqrt{3}-10\sqrt{3}\)

Answer: \(2\sqrt{3}\)

7.5

Multiplying Radical Expressions:

Simplify \(4\sqrt{8}*\sqrt{5}\)

Multiply the coefficients together and the radicals together: \((4*1)*(\sqrt{8*5})\)

Simplify: \(4\sqrt{40}\)

Answer: \(8\sqrt{10}\)

Dividing Radical Expressions:

Simplify \(15\sqrt{8}*3\sqrt{2}\)

Divide the coefficients and the radicals: \(\frac{15\sqrt{8}}{3\sqrt{2}}=\frac{15}{3}*\frac{\sqrt{8}}{\sqrt{2}}\)

Simplify: \(5\sqrt{4}\)

Answer: \(10\)

Rationalizing the Denominator:

Rationalize: \(\frac{2x}{\sqrt{5}-\sqrt{2}}\)

Multiply the top and bottom by the denominator, but with the opposite sign: \(\frac{2x}{\sqrt{5}-\sqrt{2}}*\frac{\sqrt{5}+\sqrt{2}}{\sqrt{5}+\sqrt{2}}\)

Simplify: \(\frac{2x*(\sqrt{5}+\sqrt{2})}{(\sqrt{5}-\sqrt{2})*(\sqrt{5}+\sqrt{2})}\)

Apply the difference of squares formula to the denominator: \(\frac{2x*(\sqrt{5}+\sqrt{2})}{(\sqrt{5}^2-\sqrt{2}^2)}\)

Answer: \(\frac{2x\sqrt{5}+2x\sqrt{2}}{3}\)

7.6

Solving Radical Equations:

Solve \(\sqrt{3-2x}=x\)

Square both sides: \(3-2x=x^2\)

Solve the polynomial: \(x^2+2x-3=0\)

\(( x-1)(x+3)=0\)

\(x=1,-3\)

Check the solutions in the original equation:

\(\sqrt{3-2x}=x, x=1\)

\(\sqrt{1}=1 \checkmark\)

\(\sqrt{3-2x}=x, x=-2\)

\(\sqrt{3-(-3x)}=-3 \)

\(\sqrt{9}=-3 \times\)

Answer: \(x=1\)
